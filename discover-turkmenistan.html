Tours & Packages

"Discover Turkmenistan" tour

Embark on a Discover Turkmenistan tour and immerse yourself in the wonders of this fascinating Central Asian country. Explore the futuristic city of Ashgabat with its stunning architecture and vibrant cityscape.

Parthian Fortresses of Nisa - UNESCO World Heritage Site

Nisa is an ancient city whose ruins are located near the village of Bagir, 18 km west of Ashgabat, the capital of Turkmenistan. It consists of two settlements - New Nisa, a Parthian city in a valley, and Old Nisa, a royal fortress on a hill.

Akhal Teke Horse Farm

Arkadash Stud Farm in Gokdepe village (50 km, 50 minutes drive) where you will have the opportunity to see, feed and ride the unique breed of Turkmen horses, Akhal-Teke. Akhal-Teke horses are a cultural treasure and historical symbol of the desert nomadic tribes of Turkmenistan. They are considered one of the oldest and rarest breeds in the world and are highly prized for their intelligence, endurance and speed.

Kov Ata - Underground lake

The underground lake Kov Ata (which means "father of caves") with a long lighted staircase of 350 steps leading to the lake is located 40 km, 40 minutes drive from Gokdepe. The locals have known about the unusual properties of this lake since time immemorial. The curative water of the lake contains hydrogen sulphide, iodine, magnesium, sodium and sulphate, as well as chlorine, aluminium, bromine, iron and antimony - a total of 27 chemical elements - because of which it has a beneficial effect on the body and improves blood circulation.

Darvaza Gas Crater

After lunch, drive north into the central Karakum Desert to see the burning Darvaza crater (260 km). On the way to the main crater, stop at the "Water" and "Sulfur Mud" craters. Upon arrival at Darvaza Crater, take a short tour of the area. The crater was formed in the early 1970s when the ground collapsed during a Soviet gas drilling expedition. Scientists reportedly set fire to the huge hole to prevent the spread of natural gas, and it has been burning ever since, making it a "must see" for those traveling to Turkmenistan.
